 7 I.        PARTIES AND JURISDICTION

 8           1.      This Court has jurisdiction over the allegations and subject matter of the People’s

 9 Complaint filed in this action, and the parties to this action; venue is proper in this County; and this

10 Court has jurisdiction to enter this Judgment. This Judgment is entered pursuant to and subject to

11 the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018, Civil Code section 1798.100 et seq., and any

12 subsequent amendments (“CCPA”).

13 II.       DEFINITIONS

14           The following terms in this Judgment shall have these meanings:

15           2.      CLEARLY AND CONSPICUOUSLY means that a required disclosure is easily

16 noticeable and easily understandable by ordinary people.

17           3.      CONSUMER has the same meaning as provided in Civil Code section 1798.140,

18 subdivision (i).

19           4.      CONTRACTOR has the same meaning as provided in Civil Code section 1798.140,

20 subdivision (j).

21           5.      EFFECTIVE DATE means the date this Judgment is entered.

22           6.      MARKETING AND RELATED SERVICES refers to services related to

23 “advertising and marketing” as defined in Civil Code section 1798.140, subdivision (a), and

24

 1 includes, without limitation, cross-context behavioral advertising and participation in a

 2 MARKETING CO-OPERATIVE.

 3           7.      MARKETING CO-OPERATIVE refers to where at least two unrelated business

 4 entities contribute the PERSONAL INFORMATION of CONSUMERS for the purpose of

 5 advertising their own products to CONSUMERS using PERSONAL INFORMATION contributed

 6 by other participating business entities.

 7           8.      NOTICE AT COLLECTION means the notice given to a CONSUMER at or before

 8 the point at which a business collects PERSONAL INFORMATION from the CONSUMER as

 9 required by Civil Code section 1798.100, subdivision (a), which may be included in a privacy

10 policy, where applicable, as permitted by implementing regulations.

11           9.      PERSONAL INFORMATION has the same meaning as provided in Civil Code

12 section 1798.140, subdivision (v), which includes the definition of “unique identifier” or “unique

13 personal identifier” as set forth in Civil Code section 1798.140, subdivision (aj).

14           10.     SALE or SELL has the same meaning as provided in Civil Code section 1798.140,

15 subdivision (ad).

16           11.     SERVICE PROVIDER has the same meaning as provided in Civil Code section

17 1798.140, subdivision (ag).

18           12.     SHARE has the same meaning as provided in Civil Code section 1798.140,

19 subdivision (ah).

20           13.     THIRD PARTY has the same meaning as provided in Civil Code section 1798.140,

21 subdivision (ai).

22 III.      INJUNCTIVE PROVISIONS

23           14.     Nothing in this Judgment alters the requirements of state or federal law to the extent

24 they offer greater protection to CONSUMERS.

 6                                         COMPLIANCE WITH LAW

 7           16.     Defendant shall comply with the following provisions of the CCPA and its

 8 implementing regulations: Civil Code sections 1798.100, 1798.115, 1798.120, 1798.130, and

 9 1798.135, and California Code Regulations title 11, sections 7010, 7011, 7012, 7013, 7024-7026,

10 and 7050-7053. Defendant shall also comply with updates and modifications to the CCPA

11 regulations related to these laws and implementing regulations.

12           17.     Defendant shall comply with the California Online Privacy Protection Act

13 (“CalOPPA”), Business and Professions Code section 22575 et seq., in connection with privacy

14 policy disclosures.

15           18.     To the extent Defendant SELLS and/or SHARES PERSONAL INFORMATION,

16 including, without limitation, through participation in a MARKETING CO-OPERATIVE,

17 Defendant shall provide notice of such SELLING and/or SHARING to CONSUMERS in its

18 privacy policy as required by Civil Code section 1798.130, subdivision (a)(5) and by Business and

19 Professions Code section 22575, and in its NOTICE AT COLLECTION. Defendant shall:

20                   a.       Include in its privacy policy and NOTICE AT COLLECTION a list of the

21 categories of PERSONAL INFORMATION that Defendant has collected about CONSUMERS and

22 SOLD and/or SHARED in the preceding 12 months; and

 2 CONSUMERS have the right to opt-out of the SALE and/or SHARING of their PERSONAL

 3 INFORMATION.

 4           19.     To the extent Defendant SELLS and/or SHARES PERSONAL INFORMATION,

 5 including, without limitation, through participation in a MARKETING CO-OPERATIVE,

 6 Defendant shall provide the required methods to opt-out of the SALE and/or SHARING of

 7 PERSONAL INFORMATION or shall otherwise comply with Civil Code section 1798.135.

 8           20.     To the extent Defendant participates in a MARKETING CO-OPERATIVE,

 9 Defendant shall CLEARLY AND CONSPICUOUSLY state in its privacy policy and NOTICE AT

10 COLLECTION that Defendant SELLS and/or SHARES PERSONAL INFORMATION by

11 participating in a MARKETING CO-OPERATIVE in which other businesses may advertise their

12 own products to the CONSUMER using PERSONAL INFORMATION collected and either

13 SHARED and/or SOLD by Defendant.

14                                         COMPLIANCE PROGRAM

15           21.     Within 180 days of the EFFECTIVE DATE, and for a period of three (3) years

16 thereafter, Defendant shall implement and maintain a compliance program to: (1) assess and

17 monitor whether it is SELLING and/or SHARING the PERSONAL INFORMATION of

18 CONSUMERS, including without limitation for MARKETING AND RELATED SERVICES or to

19 providers of analytics or measurement services, utilizing technical and operational controls, and, (2)

20 if so, evaluate whether it is effectively providing CONSUMERS with the required notices,

21 including in its privacy policy and NOTICE AT COLLECTION, and the right to opt-out.

22           22.     Defendant shall document its compliance program in writing, including its policies

23 and procedures and the technical and operational controls implemented and utilized for assessing

 6              CERTIFICATION OF COMPLIANCE TO THE ATTORNEY GENERAL

 7           23.     Within 180 days of the EFFECTIVE DATE, and annually for a period of three (3)

 8 years thereafter, Defendant shall provide a certification to the California Attorney General’s Office

 9 (i) affirming that Defendant is in compliance with this Judgment and has implemented and is

10 maintaining a compliance program consistent with the requirements set forth in Paragraphs 21 and

11 22; (ii) summarizing Defendant’s compliance program; and (iii) confirming whether or not

12 Defendant has participated in a MARKETING CO-OPERATIVE since the EFFECTIVE DATE.

13           24.     Within forty-five (45) days of receipt of a written request from the California

14 Attorney General’s Office, Defendant shall submit additional information concerning its

15 compliance with this Judgment, its compliance program set forth in Paragraphs 21 and 22, and/or

16 any participation in any MARKETING CO-OPERATIVE. These provisions shall not in any way

17 limit the Attorney General’s rights with respect to investigation and enforcement of potential

18 violations of this Judgment or controlling law.

19           25.     To the full extent permitted by the laws of the State of California, the California
