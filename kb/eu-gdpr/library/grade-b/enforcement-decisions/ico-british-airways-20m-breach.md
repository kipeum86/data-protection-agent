# ICO UK v British Airways - GBP 20 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), UK
- **Decision Date:** October 16, 2020
- **Target:** British Airways (IAG)
- **Fine:** GBP 20,000,000 (reduced from initial GBP 183M notice)
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 5(1)(f), Art. 32 GDPR

## Facts

In 2018, a Magecart-style attack compromised British Airways' website and mobile app. Attackers modified JavaScript code to skim customer payment card details during the booking process. Approximately 429,612 customers and staff were affected. Data compromised included names, addresses, payment card numbers, and CVV codes.

## Violations Found

BA failed to implement appropriate security measures:
- Inadequate monitoring of web application changes
- Lack of multi-factor authentication
- Insufficient testing of website security
- Failure to detect the breach for approximately two months

## Fine Reduction

The original Notice of Intent proposed GBP 183 million. The final fine was reduced to GBP 20 million considering:
- BA's cooperation with the investigation
- Steps taken to mitigate the breach
- Economic impact of COVID-19

## Significance

- First major GDPR fine for a data breach (web application security failure)
- Demonstrates that "appropriate" security under Art. 32 requires proactive monitoring
- Shows that breach fines can be substantially reduced through cooperation and remediation
