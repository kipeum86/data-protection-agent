# CNPD Luxembourg v Amazon - EUR 746 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Commission Nationale pour la Protection des Donnees (CNPD), Luxembourg
- **Decision Date:** July 15, 2021
- **Target:** Amazon Europe Core S.a r.l.
- **Fine:** EUR 746,000,000
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 6, Art. 5 GDPR

## Facts

The investigation originated from a complaint filed by La Quadrature du Net, a French digital rights advocacy group. The CNPD examined Amazon's practices regarding targeted advertising and the processing of personal data for behavioral advertising purposes without valid consent.

## Violations Found

Amazon failed to obtain clear permission from users before processing personal data for targeted advertising. The CNPD found:
- Amazon's reliance on "legitimate interests" to justify behavioral advertising was not legally valid
- Amazon's privacy information procedures did not comply with GDPR transparency requirements
- Inadequate consent mechanisms for ad targeting

## Legal History

The fine has had a turbulent judicial history. In March 2026, Luxembourg's Administrative Court annulled the EUR 746 million fine on procedural grounds, but endorsed the CNPD's substantive findings "almost in their entirety," specifically confirming that Amazon's use of legitimate interests for behavioral advertising was unlawful.

## Significance

- Second-largest GDPR fine at time of imposition
- Established that behavioral advertising requires explicit consent, not merely legitimate interest
- Demonstrates cross-border complaint mechanisms (French NGO triggering Luxembourg enforcement)
