# CNIL France v Criteo - EUR 40 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** CNIL (France)
- **Decision Date:** June 15, 2023
- **Target:** Criteo SA
- **Fine:** EUR 40,000,000
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 7(1), 15, 17(1), 26 GDPR

## Facts

Criteo, a French advertising technology company, collected and processed personal data through cookies placed on partner websites for retargeted advertising. Multiple complaints were filed by Privacy International and noyb.

## Violations Found

1. **Consent (Art. 7):** Criteo could not demonstrate valid consent for cookie-based tracking. Partners' consent collection mechanisms were inadequate.
2. **Right of Access (Art. 15):** Criteo failed to provide complete information about data processing when access requests were made.
3. **Right to Erasure (Art. 17):** Incomplete erasure of personal data upon request.
4. **Joint Controllership (Art. 26):** Criteo and its partners were joint controllers but had no Art. 26 arrangement specifying respective responsibilities.

## Significance

- Major enforcement action against advertising technology sector
- Establishes that ad-tech companies bear responsibility for verifying partner consent collection
- Joint controllership obligations between ad-tech platforms and publisher partners
- Reinforces that cookie consent must be demonstrably valid
