# DPC Ireland v Meta Ireland - EUR 1.2 Billion Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Data Protection Commission (DPC), Ireland
- **Decision Date:** May 22, 2023
- **Target:** Meta Platforms Ireland Limited
- **Fine:** EUR 1,200,000,000 (highest GDPR fine as of 2023)
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 46(1) GDPR

## Facts

The DPC initiated an ex officio inquiry into Meta Platforms Ireland's transfers of personal data to the United States in connection with the Facebook service. Meta had continued these transfers after the CJEU's Schrems II judgment invalidated Privacy Shield and despite the absence of an adequacy decision covering the relevant transfer framework at the time.

## Violations Found

Meta Ireland relied on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for its EU-US data transfers but failed to implement adequate supplementary measures to address the risks arising from US surveillance laws (FISA Section 702, Executive Order 12333).

## EDPB Involvement

The EDPB adopted a binding decision under Art. 65 GDPR, instructing the DPC to impose a fine. The DPC's initial draft decision was challenged by multiple concerned supervisory authorities who considered the proposed enforcement insufficient.

## Corrective Measures

1. Administrative fine of EUR 1.2 billion
2. Order to suspend data transfers to the US within 5 months
3. Order to bring processing into compliance with Chapter V GDPR within 6 months

## Significance

- Highest GDPR fine ever imposed at the time
- Demonstrates enforcement of Chapter V transfer requirements post-Schrems II
- Shows the EDPB's willingness to override lead supervisory authorities via binding decisions
- Subsequently partially mooted by the EU-US Data Privacy Framework adequacy decision (July 2023)
