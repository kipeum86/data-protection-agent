# DPC Ireland v Meta (Facebook + Instagram) - EUR 390 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Data Protection Commission (DPC), Ireland
- **Decision Date:** January 4, 2023
- **Target:** Meta Platforms Ireland Limited
- **Fine:** EUR 390,000,000 (EUR 210M for Facebook + EUR 180M for Instagram)
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 6(1)(b), Art. 5(1)(a), Art. 12, Art. 13(1)(c) GDPR

## Facts

The DPC investigated Meta's reliance on "contractual necessity" (Art. 6(1)(b)) as the legal basis for processing personal data for personalized advertising on Facebook and Instagram. Meta had embedded behavioural advertising within its terms of service, arguing that ad personalisation was necessary for performance of the user contract.

## Violations Found

1. **Art. 6(1)(b):** Personalized advertising is not objectively necessary for performing the core Facebook or Instagram service.
2. **Art. 5(1)(a), Art. 12, Art. 13(1)(c):** Meta did not transparently explain the legal basis and processing logic for behavioural advertising.

## EDPB Binding Decision

The EDPB adopted binding decisions 3/2022 and 4/2022, requiring the DPC to find additional transparency infringements and to reassess the level of sanction.

## Significance

- Definitively establishes that behavioral advertising cannot rely on "contractual necessity"
- Confirmed that the Facebook and Instagram inquiries formed a single landmark enforcement moment with a combined EUR 390 million penalty
- Demonstrates how EDPB dispute-resolution can materially reshape a lead authority's final decision
