# AP Netherlands v Uber - EUR 290 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (AP), Netherlands
- **Decision Date:** August 26, 2024
- **Target:** Uber B.V. / Uber Technologies Inc.
- **Fine:** EUR 290,000,000
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 44 GDPR

## Facts

The AP, acting as lead supervisory authority (Uber EMEA HQ in the Netherlands), investigated after the French DPA forwarded a complaint from Ligue des droits de l'Homme (LDH) on behalf of 172 Uber drivers.

Uber processed sensitive data through its app: location data, photos, payment details, ID records, and criminal and health records of drivers. This data was collected through Uber's EU entity (UBV) and transferred to US-based entity (UTI).

## Violations Found

Uber stopped using SCCs in August 2021 and continued data transfers without adequate safeguards until November 2023 (over 2 years). After that, Uber certified under the EU-US Data Privacy Framework.

## Fine Calculation

The AP considered:
- Severity: HIGH (sensitive personal data categories)
- Duration: 2 years and 3 months without valid transfer mechanism
- Categories of data: Including health, criminal records, biometric data

## Significance

- Largest fine imposed by Dutch DPA
- Demonstrates enforcement regarding gap periods between transfer mechanisms
- Sensitive data transfers receive heightened scrutiny
- Cross-border complaint mechanism (French NGO, Dutch enforcement)
