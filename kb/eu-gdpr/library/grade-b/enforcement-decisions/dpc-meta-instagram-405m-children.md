# DPC Ireland v Meta (Instagram) - EUR 405 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Data Protection Commission (DPC), Ireland
- **Decision Date:** September 5, 2022
- **Target:** Meta Platforms Ireland Ltd (Instagram)
- **Fine:** EUR 405,000,000
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 5(1)(a), 5(1)(c), 6(1), 12(1), 24, 25(1), 25(2), 35(1) GDPR

## Facts

The inquiry was initiated on September 21, 2020, following information provided by a US data scientist (David Stier) and issues identified by the DPC during examination of Instagram's user registration process. The investigation concerned processing of personal data relating to child users of Instagram.

## Violations Found

1. **Public disclosure of children's data:** Email addresses and phone numbers of children using Instagram's business account feature were publicly displayed
2. **Public-by-default settings:** Personal Instagram accounts of children were set to public by default
3. **Failure of data protection by design (Art. 25):** Instagram did not implement appropriate privacy-protective default settings for child users
4. **Missing DPIA (Art. 35):** No Data Protection Impact Assessment conducted for processing involving children's data
5. **Transparency failures (Art. 12):** Inadequate information provided to child users

## EDPB Involvement

The EDPB adopted a binding decision on July 28, 2022, which upheld objections requiring the DPC to amend its draft decision to include an Article 6(1) infringement finding and reassess proposed fines.

## Significance

- Landmark enforcement regarding children's data under GDPR
- Demonstrates that "public by default" settings are incompatible with data protection by design (Art. 25)
- EDPB intervention increased both the scope of violations found and the fine amount
