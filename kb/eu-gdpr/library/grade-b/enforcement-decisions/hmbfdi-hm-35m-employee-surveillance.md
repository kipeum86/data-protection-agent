## HmbBfDI v H&M — EUR 35.3 Million

**DPA:** HmbBfDI (Hamburg, Germany)

**Target Entity:** H&M Hennes & Mauritz Online Shop A.B. & Co. KG

**Fine Amount:** EUR 35,258,708

**Decision Date:** 2020-10-01

**Significance:** landmark

### Summary

Hamburg DPA fined H&M EUR 35.3M for systematically surveilling employees at its Nuremberg service centre since at least 2014. Managers recorded employees' vacation details, illness symptoms, diagnoses, and religious beliefs after 'Welcome Back Talks,' storing this data where up to 50 managers could access it. The landmark decision established strict limits on employer processing of employee personal data, particularly health information.

### Violated Articles

Art. 5(1)(a), Art. 5(1)(b), Art. 5(1)(c), Art. 6(1), Art. 9(1)
