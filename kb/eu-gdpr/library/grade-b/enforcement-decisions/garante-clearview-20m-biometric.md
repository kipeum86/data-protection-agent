# Garante v Clearview AI - EUR 20 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Garante per la protezione dei dati personali (Italy)
- **Target:** Clearview AI Inc.
- **Fine:** EUR 20,000,000
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 6, 9, 14 GDPR

## Facts

Clearview AI scraped facial images from publicly accessible websites and built a biometric database used for facial recognition services. The Italian Garante found that this mass collection and reuse of images lacked a valid legal basis and unlawfully processed special-category biometric data of individuals located in Italy.

## Violations Found

1. **No legal basis (Art. 6):** No valid legal basis for mass scraping of facial images
2. **Special categories (Art. 9):** Biometric data processed without any Art. 9(2) exception
3. **Right to information (Art. 14):** No information provided to data subjects whose images were scraped

## Significance

- One of the earliest major European enforcement actions against facial-recognition scraping
- Established that publicly accessible photos cannot be repurposed into a biometric surveillance database without a lawful basis
- Similar sanctions later followed in France, Greece, the Netherlands, and the United Kingdom
