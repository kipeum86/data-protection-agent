# DPC Ireland v TikTok - EUR 530 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Data Protection Commission (DPC), Ireland
- **Decision Date:** May 2, 2025
- **Target:** TikTok Technology Limited
- **Fine:** EUR 530,000,000
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 46(1), Art. 13(1)(f) GDPR

## Facts

The DPC investigated TikTok's transfers of EEA user personal data to China. TikTok initially claimed EEA user data was not stored on Chinese servers, but corrected this in April 2025, admitting that "limited EEA User Data had in fact been stored on servers in China."

## Violations Found

**Article 46(1) - Unlawful transfers (EUR 485M):** TikTok failed to verify, guarantee and demonstrate that supplementary measures and SCCs were effective to ensure essentially equivalent protection when transferring data to China via remote access.

**Article 13(1)(f) - Transparency failure (EUR 45M):** TikTok's October 2021 privacy policy failed to name China as a transfer destination and did not specify that personnel in China could remotely access data stored elsewhere.

## Corrective Measures

TikTok must bring processing into compliance within 6 months or face suspension of all data transfers to China.

## Significance

- Demonstrates enforcement of Chapter V beyond US transfers (China)
- "Remote access" from third countries constitutes a transfer under GDPR
- Transparency obligations require specific identification of transfer destinations
- Misrepresentation to the DPA (initially denying Chinese storage) noted as aggravating factor
