# DPC Ireland v LinkedIn - EUR 310 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Data Protection Commission (DPC), Ireland
- **Decision Date:** October 24, 2024
- **Target:** LinkedIn Ireland Unlimited Company
- **Fine:** EUR 310,000,000
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 6(1), Art. 5(1)(a) GDPR

## Facts

Following an August 2018 complaint by French NGO La Quadrature Du Net, the DPC investigated LinkedIn's use of member personal data for behavioral analysis and targeted advertising. LinkedIn relied on consent, legitimate interest, and contractual necessity as legal bases.

## Violations Found

The DPC concluded LinkedIn failed to meet the applicable thresholds for each claimed legal basis:
- **Consent:** Not validly obtained for advertising purposes
- **Legitimate interest:** The rights and freedoms of members outweighed LinkedIn's commercial interests
- **Contractual necessity:** Behavioral advertising was not necessary for the performance of the LinkedIn service contract

## Corrective Measures

1. Administrative fine of EUR 310 million
2. Reprimand
3. Order to bring processing into compliance

## Significance

- Reinforces that behavioral advertising requires valid consent, not merely legitimate interest
- Large platforms cannot rely on contractual necessity for non-essential advertising features
- Cross-border complaint mechanism (French NGO, Irish DPC enforcement)
