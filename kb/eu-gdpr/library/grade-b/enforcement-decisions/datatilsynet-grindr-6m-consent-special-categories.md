## Datatilsynet v Grindr — EUR 5.7 Million (NOK 65M)

**DPA:** Datatilsynet (Norway)

**Target Entity:** Grindr LLC

**Fine Amount:** NOK 65,000,000 (approx. EUR 5,700,000)

**Decision Date:** 2021-12-13

**Significance:** landmark

### Summary

Norway's DPA fined Grindr NOK 65M for sharing users' precise GPS locations and user profile data — which inherently reveals sexual orientation (a special category) — with advertising partners without valid GDPR consent. Users were forced to accept the privacy policy in its entirety to use the app, rendering consent non-freely-given. The Norwegian Court of Appeal upheld the fine in 2025, confirming that app data inherently revealing special categories requires explicit consent.

### Violated Articles

Art. 6(1), Art. 9(1)
