# CJEU C-210/16: Wirtschaftsakademie (Facebook Fan Page Joint Controllership)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Date:** June 5, 2018
- **ECLI:** EU:C:2018:388

## Facts

A company created and administered a Facebook fan page for educational services. Through Facebook Insights, the company received statistical data about page visitors without explicit user notification. Facebook used cookies containing unique user codes (active for two years) to collect personal information from visitors.

## Key Holdings

1. **Mere use is not controlling:** Simply using a social network platform does not automatically make a user a controller.

2. **Joint controllership applies when:** An administrator who creates the page, accepts terms, sets objectives, and promotes activities "has an influence on the processing of personal data" for statistical reporting purposes.

3. **Shared but unequal responsibility:** Joint controllers with the platform may bear different levels of responsibility depending on their involvement stages and degrees.

## Significance

Clarifies data controller liability for social media administrators. Platform involvement combined with page governance creates joint responsibility under data protection law. Foundation for the broader Art. 26 joint controller doctrine.
