# CJEU C-582/14: Breyer (Dynamic IP Addresses)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Citation:** ECLI:EU:C:2016:779
- **Date:** October 19, 2016

## Facts

Mr. Breyer visited publicly accessible websites operated by German Federal Institutions. These sites logged access activities including dynamic IP addresses. Mr. Breyer sought a court order preventing the Federal Republic from storing his access information.

## Key Holdings

**Dynamic IP Addresses as Personal Data:** A dynamic IP address alone is not information about an identified person. However, it constitutes personal data where "an online media services provider has legal means that enable them to identify natural persons." The Court referenced Recital 26 of Directive 95/46, requiring consideration of "all the means likely reasonably to be used either by the controller or by any other person to identify the said person."

**Lawfulness of Processing:** The Court struck down Germany's Paragraph 15 of the Telemedia Act (TMG), finding it impermissibly restricted the lawfulness conditions by excluding necessary balancing of controller interests against "the interests, fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject."

## Significance

Clarified that dynamic IP addresses qualify as personal data when controllers possess or can feasibly obtain identifying information. National laws cannot unilaterally narrow the consent and legitimate interest requirements under EU data protection principles.
