# CJEU C-413/23: EDPS v SRB (Pseudonymised Data)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Case Number:** C-413/23 P
- **Decision Date:** 2025

## Facts

During the 2017 resolution of Banco Popular Espanol, the Single Resolution Board (SRB) invited shareholders and creditors to submit comments. SRB pseudonymised these submissions by removing names and replacing them with randomly generated codes before transferring them to a consulting firm. Several participants complained to the EDPS that they had not been informed of this data transfer.

## Key Holdings

**Relative Approach to Personal Data:** The CJEU explicitly confirmed that sufficiently strongly pseudonymised data may constitute personal data for the original controller but NOT for the recipient who cannot reverse the pseudonymisation and cannot identify data subjects by other means.

**Contextual Assessment:** The categorisation of personal data as pseudonymous or anonymous should be assessed from the relative perspective of the recipient/holder of that data. Data could be pseudonymous in the hands of one party (the discloser, with access to re-identification information) and anonymous in the hands of another.

**Transparency Obligations:** "For the purposes of applying the obligation to provide information, the identifiable nature of the data subject must be assessed at the time of collection of the data and from the point of view of the controller at that point."

## Significance

First CJEU judgment explicitly addressing the legal status of pseudonymised data. Adopts a relative approach -- whether data is "personal" depends on who holds it and what re-identification means are available to them. Major implications for data sharing, research, and controller/processor arrangements.
