# CJEU C-300/21: Osterreichische Post (Non-Material Damages)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Case Number:** C-300/21
- **Decision Date:** May 4, 2023
- **ECLI:** EU:C:2023:370

## Facts

Austrian Postal Service collected data on citizens' political affiliations based on socio-demographic criteria. The service generated statistics inferring that a specific individual held strong affinities with a particular Austrian political party, without consent. The individual claimed distress, loss of confidence, and feeling exposed.

## Key Holdings

**Three Cumulative Conditions:** Article 82 GDPR requires: (1) GDPR infringement, (2) demonstrable damage, and (3) causal connection. A bare violation alone is insufficient.

**No Seriousness Threshold:** "Article 82 GDPR does not include any reference to a threshold of seriousness." National thresholds conflicting with this principle are precluded. The concept of "damage" must be interpreted broadly.

**Compensatory Function:** Member states determine compensation assessment rules (quantum), provided they comply with equivalence and effectiveness principles. Full compensation is required; punitive damages are unnecessary.

## Significance

Data subjects can recover for non-material damages without proving severe psychological harm. Substantially broadens Article 82 protections across EU member states. First CJEU ruling on non-material damages under GDPR.
