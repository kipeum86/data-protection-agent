# CJEU C-673/17: Planet49 (Cookie Consent)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Case Number:** C-673/17
- **ECLI:** EU:C:2019:801
- **Date:** October 1, 2019

## Facts

Planet49 operated an online lottery requiring participants to provide name and address. Below the input fields were two checkboxes: one for promotional contact (not pre-ticked) and one for cookie placement (pre-ticked). Participation required ticking at least the first box. The German Consumer Federation challenged whether this consent mechanism satisfied legal requirements.

## Key Holdings

**Consent Requirements:** Valid consent demands "a clear affirmative act." Pre-ticked boxes requiring users to opt-out are insufficient. "Inaction is insufficient to establish whether the consent is a freely given and informed decision."

**Information Requirements:** Service providers must provide information including "the duration of the operation of cookies and whether or not third parties may have access to those cookies."

**Scope:** The consent obligation applies regardless of whether collected data qualifies as personal data.

**Exception:** "Strictly necessary" cookies for requested services remain exempt from consent requirements.

## Significance

Clarified and harmonized consent standards across three data protection frameworks (Directive 95/46, GDPR, ePrivacy Directive). Established that passive consent mechanisms violate user privacy protections, requiring explicit, affirmative user action.
