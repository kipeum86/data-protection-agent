# CJEU C-340/21: Natsionalna agentsia za prihodite (Data Breach Liability)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Case Number:** C-340/21
- **Decision Date:** December 14, 2023
- **ECLI:** EU:C:2023:986

## Facts

The Bulgarian Tax Agency suffered a cyberattack resulting in a data breach exposing personal information of over 6 million individuals. The complainant sued for approximately EUR 510 in compensation for non-material damages.

## Key Holdings

### On Technical Measures (Questions 1-2)
A data breach does not automatically indicate inadequate TOMs. The GDPR establishes a "risk management system" rather than eliminating all breach risks. Controllers must implement security "appropriate to the risk" without guaranteeing absolute prevention.

### On Burden of Proof (Question 3)
**The controller bears the burden** of proving TOM adequacy. The accountability principle in Articles 5(2) and 24 GDPR establishes this allocation. Placing this burden on data subjects would render Article 82 compensation right ineffective.

### On Third-Party Causation (Question 4)
Controllers cannot escape liability simply because third parties caused the breach. Article 82(2) permits exemption only when controllers demonstrate the damaging act was "not attributable" to them.

### On Non-Material Damages (Question 5)
**Fear of potential data misuse constitutes compensable non-material damage.** Article 82(1) does not require actual misuse before compensation claims arise. The mere loss of data control suffices. However, fears must be reasonable and connected to specific circumstances.

## Significance

Combined with C-300/21, substantially facilitates class action litigation for data breaches and shifts litigation risk toward controllers. Clarifies burden of proof for security adequacy.
