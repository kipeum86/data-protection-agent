# CJEU C-687/21: MediaMarktSaturn (Non-Material Damages Proof Standard)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Case Number:** C-687/21
- **Decision Date:** January 25, 2024
- **ECLI:** EU:C:2024:72

## Facts

A customer purchased a household appliance from Saturn Media Market using a payment contract containing personal data (name, address, employer, income, bank details). At checkout, a third party mistakenly collected both the appliance and contract. Saturn employees recovered both items within 30 minutes. No evidence showed the third party misused the data.

## Key Holdings

**Non-Material Damages Standard:** The claimant must prove a "well founded fear and a real risk of misuse" of personal data, not merely hypothetical concerns.

**Burden of Proof:**
- Claimant proves: GDPR infringement and actual damage
- Controller proves: adequacy of technical/organizational security measures
- Causation is presumed unless controller proves otherwise

**Breach Alone Insufficient:** Unauthorized third-party access does not automatically prove inadequate security measures (referencing C-340/21).

**Severity Irrelevant:** The seriousness of the infringement does not determine compensation amounts.

**Compensatory Function:** Article 82 operates compensatorily, not punitively.

## Significance

Consolidates GDPR damages jurisprudence. Establishes rigorous proof standards for non-material damages claims while clarifying respective burdens between claimants and controllers.
