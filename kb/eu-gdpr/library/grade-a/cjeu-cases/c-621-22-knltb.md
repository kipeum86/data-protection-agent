# CJEU C-621/22: KNLTB (Commercial Legitimate Interest)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Decision Date:** October 4, 2024
- **ECLI:** EU:C:2024:857

## Facts

A Dutch tennis federation provided member personal data to two sponsors -- a sporting equipment retailer and an online betting company -- in exchange for payment. The sponsors used this data for marketing purposes without member consent. The Dutch DPA (AP) imposed a fine of EUR 525,000.

## Key Holdings

**Key Principle:** "A legitimate interest does not have to follow from a legal norm but it must be lawful."

- Legitimate interests need not be enshrined in law; they may encompass commercial purposes and direct marketing
- However, processing must be necessary and proportionate
- A balancing test between controller interests and data subject rights is mandatory
- Members could not reasonably have foreseen data disclosure to gambling operators at enrollment
- Less restrictive alternatives (prior consent seeking) existed

## Significance

Clarifies that legitimate interest under Article 6(1)(f) GDPR extends beyond legally-determined interests to include business purposes, provided necessity and proportionality requirements are satisfied. Challenges narrow DPA interpretations.
