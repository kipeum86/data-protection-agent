# CJEU C-136/17: GC and Others (De-referencing of Sensitive Data)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Decision Date:** September 24, 2019
- **ECLI:** EU:C:2019:773

## Facts

Four individuals sought de-referencing of search engine links pointing to third-party websites containing their sensitive personal data. Google rejected these requests. CNIL declined to order Google's compliance. The matter escalated to France's Council of State.

## Key Holdings

**Search Engine Classification:** Search engine activities constitute "personal data processing," making operators "controllers" under Article 4(7).

**Sensitive Data Definition:** Information about legal proceedings and criminal convictions qualifies as sensitive data under "offences" and "criminal convictions" categories (Article 10 GDPR).

**De-referencing Requirements:** Search engine operators must generally honor de-referencing requests for sensitive data pages unless exemptions apply.

**Balancing Test Requirement:** Operators need not automatically remove links. They must conduct a "balancing test of the competing rights in presence" when evaluating sensitive data removal requests.

## Significance

Extends Google Spain principles specifically to sensitive personal data categories. Establishes a nuanced framework requiring case-by-case balancing rather than automatic removal.
