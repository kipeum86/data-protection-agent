# CJEU C-154/21: Osterreichische Post (Right of Access - Recipients)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Citation:** ECLI:EU:C:2023:3
- **Date:** January 12, 2023

## Facts

An Austrian data subject requested information from the Austrian Post about their personal data and whether it had been shared with third parties. The controller provided only generic descriptions of recipient categories (charities, NGOs, political parties) without disclosing specific identities.

## Key Holdings

Controllers must disclose the actual identity of specific recipients of personal data. This obligation stems from:
- The right of access under Article 15 GDPR, which enables exercise of other data subject rights
- The transparency principle (Article 5(1)(a) GDPR)
- Article 19 GDPR requiring controllers to inform recipients of data subject requests

**Exception:** Controllers may limit disclosure to recipient categories when specific recipients cannot yet be identified, or the request qualifies as "manifestly unfounded or excessive" under Article 12(5)(b).

## Significance

Substantially strengthens data subject rights by establishing that access requests must yield specific recipient identities rather than vague categorical information.
