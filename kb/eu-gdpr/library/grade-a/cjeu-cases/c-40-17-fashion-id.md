# CJEU C-40/17: Fashion ID (Social Plugin Joint Controllership)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Case Number:** C-40/17
- **Decision Date:** July 29, 2019
- **ECLI:** EU:C:2019:629

## Facts

Fashion ID, an online clothing retailer, embedded Facebook's 'Like' plug-in on its website. This plug-in transmitted visitor personal data to Facebook Ireland automatically -- regardless of whether visitors clicked it or had Facebook accounts. The transmission occurred without user knowledge or explicit consent.

## Key Holdings

**Joint Controller Status:** Fashion ID was a joint controller with Facebook Ireland. Fashion ID deliberately embedded the plug-in to increase product visibility and commercial advantage, knowing data transmission would occur. Lack of direct data access did not negate controller status.

**Scope of Liability:** Fashion ID's liability extended to the "transmission of personal data" and failure to disclose this activity -- but limited to the data collection and transmission stages (not subsequent processing by Facebook).

**Legal Standing:** Articles 22 and 24 of Directive 95/46 authorize consumer protection associations to bring proceedings against data protection violations.

## Significance

Website operators embedding third-party social plug-ins share controller responsibility for resulting data processing, regardless of direct data access. Companies must obtain proper consent and provide transparency before implementing such tools.
