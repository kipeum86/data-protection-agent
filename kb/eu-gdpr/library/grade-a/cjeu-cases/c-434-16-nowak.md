# CJEU C-434/16: Nowak (Exam Answers as Personal Data)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Citation:** ECLI:EU:C:2017:994
- **Date:** December 20, 2017

## Facts

Peter Nowak, a trainee accountant, failed a professional examination for the fourth time. He submitted a data access request to the Institute of Chartered Accountants in Ireland (CAI). The CAI refused to disclose his exam script, claiming it contained no personal data.

## Key Holdings

Written exam answers and examiner comments "constitute information relating to that candidate" because:
- The answers reflect the candidate's knowledge and competence
- They evaluate professional abilities with consequences for career prospects
- They determine individual performance

The Court emphasized the Directive's "very wide" scope and concluded that excluding exam scripts would eliminate mandatory data protections and subject access rights.

## Significance

Broadly interprets what qualifies as personal data. Protects candidates' access to examination materials. Establishes data protection safeguards in educational and professional credentialing contexts.
