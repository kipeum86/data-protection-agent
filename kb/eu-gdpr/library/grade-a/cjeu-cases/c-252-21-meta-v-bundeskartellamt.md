# CJEU C-252/21: Meta Platforms v Bundeskartellamt

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Decision Date:** July 4, 2023
- **Citation:** ECLI:EU:C:2023:537

## Facts

Meta operates Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp. The German Federal Cartel Office (Bundeskartellamt) prohibited Meta from making Facebook use conditional on processing "off-Facebook data" collected across Meta's services and third-party websites via cookies and tracking technologies. Users must accept general terms of use to access Facebook.

## Key Holdings

**Competition Authority Jurisdiction:** National competition authorities may examine GDPR compliance when necessary to establish dominance abuse, provided they respect supervisory authority decisions and coordinate appropriately.

**Special Category Data (Art. 9(1)):** Tracking visits to websites containing sensitive topics through cookies constitutes processing of "special categories of personal data" under Article 9(1), subject to strict derogation requirements.

**Manifest Publicity Exception:** Users do not "manifestly make public" data collected via tracking technologies.

**Necessity Under Article 6(1)(b):** Processing must be "objectively indispensable" -- essential to contract performance. Personalized content or seamless multi-service integration may not meet this threshold.

**Legitimate Interest (Art. 6(1)(f)):** Personalized advertising financing cannot justify processing absent consent.

**Consent Validity:** While dominant position does not automatically invalidate consent, it substantially affects user freedom. The imbalance created by dominance is critical to freely-given consent assessment.

## Significance

Clarifies that competition and data protection authorities can work within overlapping jurisdictions. Establishes strict interpretations of GDPR justifications for major platforms. Market dominance fundamentally complicates consent validity.
